Success Story March 10, 2026 By Sarah Nalwoga

How Bwaise Community Reduced Flooding by 60% Through Collective Action

In the heart of Kawempe Division, Bwaise Parish has long been synonymous with flooding. For years, seasonal rains would turn streets into rivers, submerge homes, and disrupt thousands of lives. But today, the story is different. Through coordinated community action led by TODFCO, Bwaise has achieved a remarkable 60% reduction in flood impact over the past year.

The transformation began with a simple realization: flooding wasn't just a natural disaster—it was a community problem that required community solutions. "We used to wait for the government or NGOs to help us," explains James Mugisha, a community leader and TODFCO volunteer. "But TODFCO taught us that we have the power to change our situation ourselves."

The approach was systematic and multi-faceted. First, volunteers conducted detailed risk mapping to identify the most vulnerable areas and drainage bottlenecks. Then, they organized monthly drainage clearing campaigns, mobilizing over 100 community members to remove waste, silt, and debris from critical water channels.

But the real breakthrough came with household-level interventions. Families learned to elevate their doorsteps, create sandbag barriers, and protect their belongings. "Before, when it rained heavily, water would enter our house and destroy everything," recounts Grace Nakato, a mother of three. "Now, we know how to protect our home. Even during the heaviest rains last month, our house stayed dry."

The impact has been transformative. Children no longer miss school due to flooding. Small businesses can operate year-round. Health risks from waterborne diseases have significantly decreased. Perhaps most importantly, the community now feels empowered rather than helpless in the face of seasonal challenges.
