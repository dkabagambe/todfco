About TODFCO

Together for Disaster Free Communities - Empowering Kawempe Division to build resilience against disasters

Who We Are

Together for Disaster Free Communities (TODFCO) is a registered Community-Based Organization operating in Kawempe Division under the jurisdiction of Kampala Capital City Authority (KCCA), Uganda. The organization is non-political, non-profit, and non-religious. Our operations are community-driven and grounded in principles of accountability, inclusiveness, and transparency.

Established in 2023, TODFCO was founded in response to the recurring floods and increasing disaster risks affecting Bwaise Parish and surrounding communities. Kawempe Division, particularly Bwaise Parish, is characterized by dense settlement patterns, informal housing structures, and valley-based terrain that makes it highly vulnerable to seasonal flooding.

We work directly with community members to strengthen their capacity to understand disaster risks, reduce vulnerabilities, and respond effectively when disasters occur. Our approach combines traditional knowledge with modern disaster management practices to create comprehensive solutions that address the unique challenges faced by urban slum communities in Uganda.

Our Vision

A disaster-resilient community where lives, property, and livelihoods are protected and sustainably developed.

🎯

Our Mission

To empower communities in Kawempe Division to prevent, prepare for, respond to, and recover from disasters through awareness, capacity building, advocacy, and community action.

📋

Our Objectives

  • Promote disaster risk reduction and preparedness at community level
  • Create awareness on environmental protection and climate change adaptation
  • Support vulnerable groups affected by disasters
  • Build community capacity in emergency response and resilience
  • Work in partnership with government, NGOs, and other stakeholders

Governance Structure

Transparent and accountable leadership guiding our mission

General Assembly

+

The supreme decision-making body comprising all registered members of TODFCO. Meets annually to approve policies, budgets, and strategic plans. Consists of registered members, founding members, executive committee members, and honorary members.

Board of Directors

+

Seven-member board elected by the General Assembly, responsible for strategic oversight, policy implementation, and resource mobilization. Provides governance oversight and strategic guidance.

Executive Director

+

Chief executive officer responsible for day-to-day operations, program implementation, staff management, and stakeholder relations. Undertakes overall leadership and coordination of organizational activities.

Programs and Operations

+

Coordinates all disaster risk reduction programs, community training, emergency response, and recovery activities. Managed by project officers responsible for core disaster risk management activities.

Administration and Finance

+

Manages financial operations, human resources, compliance, and organizational development. Ensures financial management and internal administrative efficiency.

Partnerships and Advocacy

+

Builds and maintains relationships with government agencies, NGOs, donors, and community stakeholders. Handles liaison, coordination, and stakeholder engagement.

Membership

Become part of our disaster resilience movement

Membership Categories

+

Ordinary Members: Community residents committed to disaster resilience.

Executive Members: Active participants in organizational leadership.

Honorary Members: Individuals recognized for outstanding contributions (by invitation).

Rights of Members

+
  • Participate in General Assembly meetings
  • Vote on organizational matters
  • Access training and capacity building programs
  • Receive priority in emergency response assistance
  • Elect and be elected into leadership positions

Duties of Members

+
  • Participate in community disaster preparedness activities
  • Share knowledge and skills with community members
  • Support emergency response operations
  • Promote TODFCO's mission and values
  • Uphold the constitution
  • Pay agreed membership fees (if any)

Volunteers and Interns

+

We welcome volunteers and interns who want to contribute to disaster resilience efforts. Opportunities include community outreach, training facilitation, research, and administrative support.

Volunteers and interns can attend and vote in general meetings during their tenure and participate in organization activities as determined by their supervisors. They are not subjected to membership fees.
