Our Services

Comprehensive disaster management solutions for resilient communities

Disaster Risk Reduction

Structured community education on disaster risk identification and preparedness

TODFCO conducts structured community trainings based on the Disaster Management Cycle. We teach the four key components:

Prevention

Reducing exposure to hazards through environmental management and infrastructure improvements

Mitigation

Minimizing the impact of potential disasters through preparedness measures and community planning

Preparedness

Ensuring that communities are ready to act before disaster strikes through training and planning

Response and Recovery

Helping restore normalcy while strengthening future resilience through coordinated response

Our trainings translate technical disaster management concepts into simple, locally relevant practices that households can apply immediately.

Flood Preparedness and Emergency Response

Strengthening household and community readiness for flood emergencies

🌊

Flood Risk Awareness

Community education on identifying flood-prone areas, understanding early warning signs, and recognizing different flood types in Bwaise.

🏠

Household Flood Protection

Practical measures including raising doorsteps, using sandbags, elevating household items, and improving rainwater diversion systems.

🚶

Evacuation Planning

Community evacuation route mapping, safe zone identification, and emergency assembly point establishment for coordinated response.

Emergency Response Operations

Flooding remains the most common and destructive hazard affecting Bwaise Parish and nearby communities. Due to the valley terrain and high population density, even moderate rainfall can lead to severe flooding.

During flood emergencies, TODFCO mobilizes community volunteers to conduct rapid assessments and identify the most vulnerable households. We coordinate fundraising efforts and collaborate with partners to provide humanitarian assistance. While we prioritize preparedness, we also recognize the importance of timely support for affected families.

Our response approach combines community mobilization with structured coordination to ensure assistance reaches those most in need.

Climate Change and Environmental Protection

Environmental awareness and climate adaptation strategies for disaster resilience

Climate variability has intensified rainfall patterns in Kampala. Environmental degradation, including blocked drainage channels and wetland encroachment, further increases flood risk.

TODFCO conducts community awareness sessions on climate change adaptation and environmental protection. We encourage responsible waste disposal, drainage protection, and sustainable settlement practices. Environmental awareness is not treated as a separate issue but as an integral component of disaster risk reduction.

♻️

Waste Management

Community clean-up campaigns and proper waste disposal to prevent drainage blockage

🌿

Wetland Protection

Promoting wetland conservation as natural flood buffers and water management systems

🌍

Climate Awareness

Educating communities on climate change impacts and adaptation strategies

Community Capacity Building

Strengthening local skills and knowledge for sustainable disaster resilience

Youth Volunteer Programs

We established volunteer programs where young people in the community play an active role in environmental protection, including waste management awareness campaigns and neighborhood clean-ups. Their participation has strengthened long-term behavioural change and reduced environmental contributors to flooding.

Disaster Preparedness Drills

Skills Training Workshops

Hands-on training sessions covering first aid, basic rescue techniques, emergency communication, and post-disaster recovery skills.

Community Leadership Development

Building local leadership capacity to coordinate disaster response, manage resources, and maintain community resilience initiatives.

Humanitarian Response

Emergency Response

Relief Distribution

Our Recovery Approach

Our humanitarian response is guided by principles of community participation, local ownership, and sustainability. We work closely with local leaders, government agencies, and partner organizations to ensure coordinated and effective response.

Through these combined efforts, TODFCO has helped shift Bwaise from a cycle of repeated crisis to a growing culture of preparedness and resilience. Flood impacts have reduced in severity in areas where interventions are sustained, and community confidence in managing disaster risks has significantly improved.

We continue to believe that lasting change begins with the people most affected — and together, we are building safer, stronger, and more resilient communities.
