Who We Are

Together for Disaster Free Communities (TODFCO) is a not-for-profit Community-Based Organization operating in Kawempe Division, Kampala, Uganda. Established in 2023, TODFCO was founded in response to the recurring floods and increasing disaster risks affecting Bwaise Parish and surrounding communities.

We work directly with community members to strengthen their capacity to understand disaster risks, reduce vulnerabilities, and respond effectively when disasters occur. Our approach emphasizes prevention, preparedness, and long-term resilience rather than emergency reaction.

Our Approach

Practical, community-led solutions for disaster resilience

🚰

Community Drainage Improvement

Monthly drainage clearing campaigns to remove plastic waste, silt, and debris blocking water channels.

🏠

Household Flood Protection

Raising doorsteps, using sandbags, elevating household items, and improving rainwater diversion.

🚨

Early Warning Systems

Localized early warning using mobile networks, notice boards, and community leaders for timely alerts.

🎓

Disaster Preparedness Training

Regular workshops on flood risk mapping, evacuation procedures, first aid, and emergency response.

How We Work

Our systematic approach to building disaster resilience

1

Community Awareness

Educating communities about disaster risks and prevention strategies through workshops and outreach.

2

Risk Mapping

Identifying and mapping high-risk areas to prioritize interventions and allocate resources effectively.

3

Training

Building local capacity through disaster preparedness training, emergency response drills, and skill development.

4

Emergency Response

Rapid mobilization of volunteers and resources to assist affected communities during disaster events.

5

Recovery

Supporting communities to rebuild and recover while strengthening resilience for future disasters.
